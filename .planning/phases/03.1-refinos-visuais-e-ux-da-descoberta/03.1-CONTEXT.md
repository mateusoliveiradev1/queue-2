# Phase 03.1: Refinos Visuais e UX da Descoberta - Context

**Gathered:** 2026-06-04T16:05:00-03:00
**Status:** Ready for planning

<domain>
## Phase Boundary

This phase turns the working Phase 3 Discovery surface into a polished QUEUE/2 product experience before the project moves into Playing Now. The phase must make `/app/descobrir` feel like the intended Tinder-like coop discovery ritual: a dominant card stage, tactile swipe decisions, compact supporting modes, a real match celebration, usable mobile behavior, and visible high-quality feedback on every control.

The scope also includes the shared app-shell and mobile foundation needed for Discovery to work on phones: authenticated navigation, button/input/control states, spacing, touch targets, focus states, and responsive tokens used by Discovery. This does not become a full redesign of every authenticated route. If the audit finds severe mobile issues outside the shared foundation touched here, record a follow-up for global mobile polish.

The phase is also a quality gate. It must resolve the known Discovery architecture debt where application use cases import presentation view models, make `check:architecture` block the regression, and prove the redesigned route in a real authenticated browser across desktop, mobile, and reduced-motion states.

</domain>

<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Tinder-like Discovery contract

- **D-01:** Phase 03.1 must deliver the Tinder-like Discovery experience now. This is a contract of acceptance, not vague inspiration.
- **D-02:** "Tinder-like" means a dominant swipe/deck ritual with a central game card, gestures, decision feedback, next-card continuity, and match-as-event. It does not mean cloning Tinder's visual identity.
- **D-03:** The first fold of `/app/descobrir` must be deck-dominant and cinematic. The active game, its cover, and the decision gesture must be the first visual signal.
- **D-04:** Replace the current administrative-feeling page with a single-card stage. The active card is the product surface; filters, modes, search, quiz, live state, and history orbit it instead of competing with it.
- **D-05:** The card uses two layers: the first view is distilled and visually strong; a secondary tray/expanded layer carries supporting facts such as platform, time, availability, source/freshness, and secondary actions.
- **D-06:** Before a match, the card focuses on the three discovery decisions: `Quero jogar`, `Agora nao`, and `Pular`. Library handoff must not compete with these primary decisions.

### Current decision behavior to preserve

- **D-07:** The current domain semantics are correct and must be preserved while improving the UI: `Quero jogar` records `want`, has positive preference weight, counts as approval, removes the card, and creates a match only when the partner also chose `want`.
- **D-08:** `Agora nao` records `not_now`, has negative preference weight, creates a 14-day cooldown, removes the card from current focus, and does not count as approval.
- **D-09:** `Pular` records `skip`, has zero preference weight, creates no cooldown, does not approve or reject the game, and advances/removes the current card without judgment.
- **D-10:** The improved UI may add optimistic/pending feedback and animation, but it must not weaken server authorization, RLS, match creation, duplicate-match protection, or decision persistence.

### Match celebration and future roulette

- **D-11:** Match animation enters this phase as a required deliverable. A match cannot be represented only by a toast or status banner.
- **D-12:** The match celebration should feel Tinder-like in immediacy and emotional clarity, but editorial QUEUE/2 in visual language: game cover, `/2`, pointer motif, sharp composition, short copy such as "Os dois quiseram", and meaningful lime/violet accents.
- **D-13:** Avoid generic confetti, random particles, meaningless glow, or arcade/casino energy. Heavy rarity spectacle remains reserved for roulette, achievements, and other explicitly heightened moments.
- **D-14:** After the match celebration, handoff actions appear for `Wishlist`, `Jogando`, or `Pausado`. `Zerado` and `Dropado` remain blocked until Phase 4 double-confirmation behavior.
- **D-15:** Discovery answers "os dois querem este jogo?". A future roulette answers "qual jogo elegivel vamos jogar agora?". The Phase 6 roulette should draw from eligible library/fila games, especially games consciously added after match/wishlist handoff.
- **D-16:** Games that were only seen, skipped, or marked `Agora nao` do not enter the future roulette pool. A match alone should not silently put a game into the roulette pool without explicit library/fila handoff.

### Motion and interaction quality

- **D-17:** Discovery must have complete, high-craft motion that makes sense for the product. This is not a minimum animation pass.
- **D-18:** Required motion moments include swipe/drag with tilt and decision state, card exit, next-card entrance, button feedback, match celebration, surprise reveal, quiz selection/result, search overlay/result selection, and Discovery loading state.
- **D-19:** Motion must serve the ritual: decide, reveal, confirm, celebrate, orient. Do not add decorative motion just to feel premium.
- **D-20:** Reduced motion is a first-class equivalent experience. It should replace heavy choreography with instant state changes, short fades, clear copy, and visible status, not remove meaning.
- **D-21:** No sound is added in this phase. Audio belongs to the future roulette/case-opening moment.

### Modes, search, filters, and handoff

- **D-22:** `Live`, `Surpresa`, `Quiz`, `Busca`, filters, history, and handoff must orbit the card. They are supporting controls around the deck, not separate panels that make Discovery feel like several apps on one page.
- **D-23:** There must not be a row of four giant mode buttons before the deck. Modes should appear as compact controls, icons, chips, or sheets attached to the card stage.
- **D-24:** Filters must not occupy the first fold as a large form. Quick filters become compact controls; advanced filters belong in a sheet/drawer or secondary layer.
- **D-25:** Search opens as an overlay/sheet over the card stage. Autocomplete stays inside the Discovery ritual, and selecting a result transforms it into the active/focused card instead of duplicating Catalog.
- **D-26:** Search must include polished states for typing, loading, empty, error, selected, focus, keyboard navigation, and reduced motion.

### Buttons, controls, and feedback

- **D-27:** Every Discovery button and interactive control must have complete, polished feedback: hover, focus-visible, pressed, loading/pending, success, error, disabled, and reduced-motion states.
- **D-28:** Server Action submissions must not feel dead. The UI needs perceptible pending/optimistic feedback where safe, and authoritative server results must still drive final state.
- **D-29:** The three deck decisions keep the labels `Quero jogar`, `Agora nao`, and `Pular`, with improved state, hierarchy, and microcopy rather than wholesale renaming.
- **D-30:** The UI must visually differentiate `Quero jogar` as positive approval, `Agora nao` as temporary/no-guilt cooldown, and `Pular` as neutral.
- **D-31:** Feedback copy should be short, ritualistic, PT-BR, and QUEUE/2-owned. Avoid generic SaaS phrasing such as "acao concluida com sucesso" and avoid constant jokes.

### Mobile-first and app shell

- **D-32:** Mobile is currently not acceptable. Phase 03.1 must treat mobile usability as a blocker, especially for Discovery.
- **D-33:** On mobile, the active card should occupy almost the whole experience. Header, mode controls, filters, search, and history must be compact, thumb-friendly, and subordinate to the card.
- **D-34:** Mobile must not use a compressed desktop sidebar. The authenticated app uses bottom navigation on mobile with clear active state, adequate touch targets, and access to `Descobrir` by thumb.
- **D-35:** Desktop can keep a sticky sidebar/rail, but it should be refined: stronger active state, better grouping, less repeated pointer noise, and a branded footer/status area that feels intentional.
- **D-36:** The small `N` visible in local screenshots is a Next.js/dev overlay indicator, not QUEUE/2 UI. It must not guide visual audit findings.
- **D-37:** The sidebar footer copy "A fila pertence aos dois. Tudo que importa aqui e compartilhado." can remain as a brand/status detail, but should feel integrated and not compete with navigation.

### Impeccable quality rubric

- **D-38:** Impeccable is mandatory as a planning and acceptance rubric for this phase, not an optional inspiration link.
- **D-39:** The planner must use Impeccable-style passes for `shape`, `animate`, `clarify`, `adapt`, `audit`, and `polish` across UI, UX, copy, motion, responsive behavior, control states, and anti-patterns.
- **D-40:** Do not install or vendor Impeccable in this phase unless explicitly requested later. The requirement is to apply the rubric and cite it in plan acceptance criteria.

### Visual anti-patterns to block

- **D-41:** Block administrative layout patterns: giant filters/forms before the deck, rows of equal CTAs competing with the card, and excessive panel/card boxes.
- **D-42:** Block generic neon/terminal styling: lime on everything, border glow by default, meaningless glow, and arcade energy outside meaningful moments.
- **D-43:** Block illegible density: text too small, cramped buttons, low touch target quality, clipped labels, overlapping UI, or mobile flows where the card is pushed away from the first action.
- **D-44:** The screen should look like a polished QUEUE/2 product, not a form dashboard and not a generic game launcher.

### Technical gates

- **D-45:** Phase completion requires a hard architecture gate. `check:architecture` must block `discovery/application -> discovery/presentation/view-models` style regressions.
- **D-46:** Browser validation is mandatory and blocking: real authenticated desktop, mobile, and reduced-motion review must pass before the phase is complete.
- **D-47:** Browser validation must explicitly inspect hydration errors, text overlap, invisible focus, broken controls, unusable mobile layout, missing animation feedback, and missing reduced-motion equivalents.
- **D-48:** Setup documentation must clearly explain migrations, VAPID, `TEST_DATABASE_URL`, `E2E_BASE_URL`, ready duo fixtures, and `E2E_PHASE3_DISCOVERY_QUERY` before Phase 4 starts.

### the agent's Discretion

- Choose exact component decomposition, animation timings, easing, CSS structure, pending-state implementation, drawer/sheet mechanics, and copy variants as long as the decisions above hold.
- Choose whether to implement optimistic visual movement before server completion or pending-then-transition after server completion, provided server state remains authoritative and failure states are clear.
- Choose exact desktop sidebar refinements within the `AppShell` scope, but do not let sidebar work consume the Discovery polish goal.

</decisions>

## Existing Code Insights

### Reusable Assets

- `DiscoveryDeck` already supports drag and keyboard decisions through the same server-backed forms. Phase 03.1 should preserve accessibility while adding better visual feedback, pending states, and card transitions.
- `DiscoveryCard` already centralizes card content, decision forms, source metadata, facts, and handoff actions. It can be redesigned into a two-layer card instead of spreading card logic into the route.
- `MatchCelebration` already exists and can become the editorial animated match event instead of inventing a disconnected surface.
- `DiscoverySearch`, `DiscoveryFilters`, `LivePanel`, `MoodQuiz`, and `MatchHistory` are existing mode surfaces that can be converted into overlays/sheets/compact orbiting controls.
- `AppShell` is the shared shell needed for desktop sticky rail refinement and mobile bottom navigation.
- `StatusToast` and `@queue/ui` feedback primitives can be reused, but Discovery feedback must not look generic or substitute for match animation.

### Established Patterns

- Authenticated routes call `requireVerifiedSession()` and route through module public APIs.
- Business rules live in domain/application and persistence in infrastructure. Routes and React components compose behavior.
- Duo-scoped data must stay authorized server-side and protected by RLS.
- RAWG attribution/source/freshness must remain visible wherever RAWG data/images appear.
- Phase 3 already treats Discovery animations and reduced motion as quality contracts.

### Integration Points

- Refactor Discovery view-model/fact builders so application use cases no longer import `../presentation/view-models`.
- Extend `scripts/check-architecture.mjs` to catch dependency inversions inside a module, at least `application -> presentation`.
- Redesign `/app/descobrir` around a card stage rather than route-level filter/mode panels.
- Introduce mobile bottom navigation in `AppShell` and keep desktop rail sticky/refined.
- Update CSS/tokens for mobile touch targets, text fit, button states, focus states, layout constraints, and reduced motion.
- Add or update browser/E2E coverage for card decisions, match animation state, search overlay, mobile layout, reduced motion, and no visual overlap.

<specifics>
## Specific Ideas

- The current screenshot was rejected because it reads like an administrative form dashboard: giant filters above the deck, equal mode buttons, many boxes, and weak card dominance.
- The desired replacement is a single-card stage where the game cover and decision gesture create the first impression.
- Keep `Quero jogar`, `Agora nao`, and `Pular` as the three main decision labels.
- Match copy should be short and ritualistic, e.g. "Os dois quiseram", "Entrou no radar da dupla", or equivalent QUEUE/2 language after `clarify`.
- Search should behave like an overlay that turns a chosen result into an active card.
- The mobile app is broadly weak today; Phase 03.1 fixes Discovery plus shared mobile foundation needed by Discovery, and records global mobile polish follow-ups if needed.
- The desktop sidebar can remain sticky and improve visually, but the local Next.js `N` dev overlay is not product UI.

</specifics>
